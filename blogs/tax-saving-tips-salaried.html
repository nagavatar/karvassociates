Frequently Asked Questions

Can I claim both 80C and 80CCD(1B) deductions? ‚ñº

Yes! Section 80C has a limit of ‚Çπ1.5 lakhs, and 80CCD(1B) provides an additional ‚Çπ50,000 deduction specifically for NPS contributions. So you can claim total ‚Çπ2 lakhs if you invest ‚Çπ1.5L in 80C instruments plus ‚Çπ50K in NPS.

I don't have rent receipts. Can I still claim HRA? ‚ñº

For HRA claims up to ‚Çπ3,000/month, rent receipts are not mandatory. Above that, you need rent receipts. For rent exceeding ‚Çπ1,00,000/year, you must provide landlord's PAN. Keep rent agreement and bank transfer records as supporting documents.

Can I switch between old and new tax regime every year? ‚ñº

Yes, salaried individuals can switch between old and new regime every year while filing ITR. However, those with business income can switch only once from new to old regime. Evaluate your deductions each year to choose the beneficial regime.

What if I forgot to submit investment proofs to employer? ‚ñº

You can still claim deductions while filing ITR. Your employer will deduct TDS based on declared investments, but actual tax will be calculated during ITR filing. If excess TDS was deducted, you'll get a refund. Keep all investment proofs ready for ITR.

Is ELSS better than PPF for tax saving? ‚ñº

ELSS: Higher returns (10-15% potential), equity risk, 3-year lock-in, LTCG tax applicable on gains > ‚Çπ1.25 lakh.
PPF: Safe returns (7.1%), no risk, 15-year lock-in, completely tax-free.
Choose ELSS for wealth creation (if risk-tolerant), PPF for safety and retirement corpus.

Can I claim tax benefit on term insurance? ‚ñº

Yes, term insurance premium qualifies for Section 80C deduction (within ‚Çπ1.5 lakh limit). Only premium up to 10% of sum assured is eligible. Death benefit received by nominee is completely tax-free under Section 10(10D).
