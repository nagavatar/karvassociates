Step-by-Step Registration Process

Create Account on Startup India Portal

Visit startupindia.gov.in and register using mobile number or email. Complete profile with basic details.

Fill Application Form

Click "Get DPIIT Recognition" and fill details: Entity name, incorporation date, industry sector, nature of business, founders' information.

Describe Your Innovation

Write a clear description (200-500 words) explaining: What problem you're solving, how your solution is innovative, scalability potential. This is crucial!

Upload Documents

Upload incorporation certificate, proof of concept, pitch deck, or product demo video. Patents/awards strengthen the application.

Self-Certification

Declare that the entity is working towards innovation and meets all eligibility criteria. Digital signature not required.

Submit & Track

Submit application. You'll receive application number for tracking. Decision usually within 2-3 working days.

Recognition Certificate

If approved, download DPIIT Recognition Certificate from portal. Valid until entity remains eligible.

  • Get incubator support letter if you're part of any recognized incubator

Frequently Asked Questions

Is DPIIT recognition and Startup India registration the same thing? ‚ñº

Yes, they refer to the same process. When you apply for Startup India recognition through the official portal, you're applying for DPIIT (Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade) recognition. Once approved, you receive a DPIIT Recognition Certificate.

Can I apply if my company is already 5 years old? ‚ñº

Yes, you can apply if your company is up to 10 years old from the date of incorporation (5 years for biotechnology startups). However, remember that the 80-IAC tax exemption allows you to claim benefits only within the first 10 years of incorporation, so applying earlier gives you more flexibility.

What if my application is rejected? Can I reapply? ‚ñº

Yes, you can reapply after addressing the reasons for rejection. Common rejection reasons include: insufficient innovation description, unclear business model, or ineligible entity type. Review the rejection feedback, strengthen your application, and resubmit. There's no limit on reapplication attempts.

Do I automatically get tax benefits after DPIIT recognition? ‚ñº

No, DPIIT recognition alone doesn't provide tax benefits. For 80-IAC tax exemption, you need a separate IMB (Inter-Ministerial Board) certification. For angel tax exemption, you need to file Form 2 on the Startup India portal. DPIIT recognition is the first step that makes you eligible to apply for these benefits.

Can a service-based company get Startup India recognition? ‚ñº

Yes, service-based companies can get recognized if they demonstrate innovation in their service delivery, technology use, or business model. Examples: SaaS platforms, AI-based services, innovative consulting models. Pure trading or brokerage businesses are excluded, but value-added services with scalability can qualify.

Is there any registration fee for Startup India? ‚ñº

No, DPIIT recognition is completely free. There's no government fee for registration. You only need to fill the online form on startupindia.gov.in and upload required documents. However, professional assistance for documentation and compliance may have associated costs.
