Complete Guide to GST Return Filing in India

GST return filing is a mandatory compliance for all registered businesses in India. Understanding which returns to file, their due dates, and the filing process can save you from penalties and interest. Here's everything you need to know.

üìä GST Returns at a Glance

Regular taxpayers must file GSTR-1 (outward supplies) + GSTR-3B (summary return) monthly/quarterly. Annual return GSTR-9 is due by December 31st of the next financial year.

Types of GST Returns

GSTR-1 Outward Supplies Return

Who files: All registered taxpayers (except composition dealers)

What to report: All sales/outward supplies made during the period

Due Date:

  • Monthly filers (turnover > ‚Çπ5 Cr): 11th of next month
  • Quarterly filers (QRMP scheme): 13th of month after quarter

Key Details to Report:

  • B2B invoices (to registered businesses) - invoice-wise
  • B2C large (> ‚Çπ2.5 lakh inter-state) - invoice-wise
  • B2C small - consolidated state-wise
  • Credit/Debit notes issued
  • Export invoices
  • Nil-rated and exempted supplies

GSTR-3B Summary Return

Who files: All regular taxpayers

What to report: Summary of outward supplies, ITC claimed, tax payable

Due Date:

  • Monthly filers: 20th of next month
  • Quarterly filers (QRMP): 22nd/24th of month after quarter

Sections in GSTR-3B:

  • 3.1 - Outward supplies and inward supplies (RCM)
  • 3.2 - Inter-state supplies to unregistered persons
  • 4 - Eligible ITC
  • 5 - Exempt, nil-rated, and non-GST supplies
  • 6.1 - Payment of tax

GSTR-9 Annual Return

Who files: Taxpayers with turnover > ‚Çπ2 crore (mandatory)

Due Date: December 31st of next financial year

What it contains:

  • Consolidated details of GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B
  • HSN-wise summary of supplies
  • ITC availed and reversed
  • Tax paid during the year
  • Demands and refunds

GSTR-9C Reconciliation Statement

Who files: Taxpayers with turnover > ‚Çπ5 crore

Due Date: December 31st (along with GSTR-9)

What it requires:

  • Reconciliation between books and GST returns
  • Certification by CA (if turnover > ‚Çπ5 Cr)
  • Self-certification allowed for turnover ‚Çπ2-5 Cr

Step-by-Step: How to File GSTR-3B

  1. Login to GST Portal

    Go to gst.gov.in, enter GSTIN and password. Navigate to Services > Returns > Returns Dashboard.

  2. Select Return Period

    Choose the financial year and month for which you're filing GSTR-3B.

  3. Fill Section 3.1 - Outward Supplies

    Enter taxable outward supplies (B2B, B2C), exempt supplies, nil-rated supplies, and inward supplies on RCM.

  4. Fill Section 4 - Input Tax Credit

    Enter ITC available from GSTR-2B auto-populated data. Verify and match with your books.

  5. Fill Section 5 - Exempt Supplies

    Enter details of exempt, nil-rated, and non-GST supplies made during the period.

  6. Review Tax Computation

    System auto-calculates tax liability. Verify CGST, SGST, IGST, and Cess amounts.

  7. Make Payment

    If tax is due, create challan and pay via net banking or NEFT/RTGS. Offset using ITC where available.

  8. File Return with DSC/EVC

    Submit return using Digital Signature Certificate or Electronic Verification Code via Aadhaar OTP.

QRMP Scheme Benefits

Quarterly Return Monthly Payment (QRMP) scheme is available for taxpayers with turnover up to ‚Çπ5 crore:

‚úÖ QRMP Advantages

  • File GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B quarterly instead of monthly
  • Pay tax monthly using PMT-06 challan
  • Use Invoice Furnishing Facility (IFF) to upload B2B invoices monthly (optional)
  • Reduces compliance burden significantly

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • GSTIN Errors: Double-check customer/vendor GSTIN before filing
  • Invoice Mismatch: Ensure GSTR-1 matches your books and GSTR-3B
  • ITC Mismatch: Claim only ITC appearing in GSTR-2B to avoid notices
  • HSN Code Errors: Use correct 4/6-digit HSN codes as applicable
  • Wrong Tax Rate: Apply correct GST rate based on HSN/SAC code
  • Missing RCM: Report reverse charge purchases in GSTR-3B
  • Late Payment: Pay tax by due date even if return is filed later

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I file GSTR-3B without filing GSTR-1? ‚ñº

No, from January 2022, GSTR-1 filing is mandatory before GSTR-3B. You cannot file GSTR-3B until GSTR-1 for that period is filed. This ensures consistency between outward supply declarations.

What is IFF and when should I use it? ‚ñº

Invoice Furnishing Facility (IFF) is optional for QRMP taxpayers to upload B2B invoices in Month 1 and Month 2 of each quarter. This helps your customers claim ITC immediately instead of waiting for quarterly GSTR-1. It's limited to invoices up to ‚Çπ50 lakhs per month.

How do I correct errors in already filed GSTR-1? ‚ñº

GSTR-1 cannot be revised once filed. Corrections must be made in the next period's GSTR-1 using Amendment tables. For invoices: use Table 9 (B2B amendments) or Table 10 (B2C amendments). For credit/debit notes: issue fresh documents in current period.

What happens if my GSTR-2B shows less ITC than my books? ‚ñº

This usually means your suppliers haven't uploaded invoices in their GSTR-1. Steps: (1) Contact supplier to file/amend GSTR-1, (2) Claim only GSTR-2B ITC in GSTR-3B to avoid notices, (3) Claim balance ITC once it appears in future GSTR-2B, (4) Keep purchase records for audit.

Is GSTR-9 (annual return) mandatory for all taxpayers? ‚ñº

GSTR-9 is mandatory only for taxpayers with aggregate turnover exceeding ‚Çπ2 crore. Below this threshold, it's optional but recommended for reconciliation. Composition dealers file GSTR-9A instead. GSTR-9C (reconciliation statement) is required for turnover > ‚Çπ5 crore.

Can I claim ITC on invoices from previous financial years? ‚ñº

As per Section 16(4), ITC for any invoice can be claimed until the earlier of: (1) November 30th of next financial year, or (2) Date of filing annual return for that year. Example: For FY 2024-25 invoices, claim ITC by November 30, 2025, or annual return date, whichever is earlier.
