GST Registration: Who Needs It & How to Apply

Goods and Services Tax (GST) registration is mandatory for businesses crossing the threshold limit or engaging in inter-state supply. This comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know about GST registration in India.

Who Must Register for GST?

GST registration is mandatory in the following cases:

üìä Turnover Threshold Limits

  • Goods Suppliers: Annual turnover exceeds ‚Çπ40 lakhs (‚Çπ20 lakhs for special category states)
  • Service Providers: Annual turnover exceeds ‚Çπ20 lakhs (‚Çπ10 lakhs for special category states)
  • Special Category States: Arunachal Pradesh, Manipur, Meghalaya, Mizoram, Nagaland, Sikkim, Tripura, Uttarakhand, Himachal Pradesh

Mandatory Registration (Regardless of Turnover)

  • Inter-state supply of goods or services
  • E-commerce operators and sellers on e-commerce platforms
  • Casual taxable persons
  • Non-resident taxable persons
  • Persons required to deduct TDS under GST
  • Input Service Distributors (ISD)
  • Agents of a supplier
  • Persons supplying goods through e-commerce aggregators

Types of GST Registration

Type Applicable To Features

Non-Resident Foreign suppliers Valid for 90 days, advance tax required

Documents Required for GST Registration

For Proprietorship

  • PAN card of proprietor
  • Aadhaar card
  • Photograph (passport size)
  • Bank account details (cancelled cheque/passbook)
  • Proof of business address (electricity bill, rent agreement)
  • NOC from landlord (if rented premises)

For Partnership/LLP

  • Partnership deed / LLP agreement
  • PAN of firm/LLP
  • PAN and Aadhaar of all partners
  • Photographs of partners
  • Address proof of principal place of business
  • Authorization letter for authorized signatory

For Private Limited Company

  • Certificate of Incorporation
  • PAN of company
  • MOA and AOA
  • Board resolution for authorized signatory
  • PAN, Aadhaar, photos of all directors
  • Address proof of registered office

Step-by-Step GST Registration Process

  1. Visit GST Portal: Go to gst.gov.in and click "Register Now"
  2. Part A - TRN Generation: Enter PAN, mobile number, email ID to generate Temporary Reference Number (TRN)
  3. Part B - Complete Application: Login with TRN and fill Form GST REG-01
  4. Business Details: Enter trade name, constitution, district, etc.
  5. Promoter/Partner Details: Add details of all stakeholders
  6. Authorized Signatory: Designate person for compliance
  7. Principal Place of Business: Upload address proof
  8. Additional Places (if any): Add branches/warehouses
  9. Bank Account Details: Link business bank account
  10. Upload Documents: All required documents as per entity type
  11. Verification: Submit using DSC, EVC, or e-Sign

‚è±Ô∏è Processing Timeline

GST registration is typically approved within 3-7 working days if all documents are in order. You'll receive GSTIN (15-digit GST Identification Number) via email and SMS.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Wrong Constitution: Selecting incorrect business type leads to rejection
  • Address Mismatch: Address on documents must match application
  • Blurry Documents: Upload clear, legible scanned copies
  • Missing NOC: For rented premises, NOC from landlord is mandatory
  • Bank Account Name: Must match the trade name or legal name
  • HSN/SAC Codes: Select correct codes for your goods/services

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does GST registration take? ‚ñº

GST registration typically takes 3-7 working days if all documents are correct. In some cases, the officer may raise queries which need to be addressed within 7 days. Using Aadhaar authentication can expedite the process to 24-48 hours.

Can I register for GST voluntarily? ‚ñº

Yes, voluntary GST registration is allowed even if your turnover is below the threshold. Benefits include claiming Input Tax Credit, business credibility, and eligibility for government tenders. Once registered, you must file returns even if there's no business activity.

What is the penalty for not registering under GST? ‚ñº

Operating without GST registration when required attracts a penalty of ‚Çπ10,000 or 10% of the tax due, whichever is higher. For deliberate tax evasion, the penalty can go up to 100% of the tax due, plus prosecution in severe cases.

Do I need separate GST for each state? ‚ñº

Yes, GST registration is state-specific. If you have business presence (office, warehouse, branch) in multiple states, you need separate GST registration in each state. However, for pure inter-state supply from one location, single registration suffices.

Can I cancel my GST registration? ‚ñº

Yes, GST registration can be cancelled if you discontinue business, turnover falls below threshold (and you want to deregister), or the business entity is sold/amalgamated. Apply using Form GST REG-16 on the portal. You must file final return (GSTR-10) within 3 months of cancellation.

What is Composition Scheme and who can opt for it? ‚ñº

Composition Scheme is for small taxpayers with turnover up to ‚Çπ1.5 crore (‚Çπ75 lakhs for service providers). Benefits include lower tax rate (1-6%), quarterly returns, and simplified compliance. However, you cannot claim ITC and cannot do inter-state supply.
