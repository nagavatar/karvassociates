Complete Guide to ITR Filing in India 2025-26

Filing Income Tax Return (ITR) is a crucial responsibility for every taxpayer in India. Whether you're a salaried individual, freelancer, business owner, or professional, understanding the ITR filing process can help you stay compliant and even claim legitimate tax refunds.

Who Should File Income Tax Return in India?

You are required to file ITR if you fall under any of these categories:

  • Income Threshold: Your gross total income exceeds ‚Çπ3,00,000 (for individuals below 60 years) before any deductions under Chapter VI-A
  • Senior Citizens (60-80 years): Income exceeds ‚Çπ3,00,000
  • Super Senior Citizens (80+ years): Income exceeds ‚Çπ5,00,000
  • Business/Profession: Any turnover from business or profession, regardless of profit or loss
  • Foreign Assets: If you hold assets or have signing authority in accounts outside India
  • High-Value Transactions: Deposited more than ‚Çπ1 crore in bank accounts, or spent more than ‚Çπ2 lakhs on foreign travel, or paid electricity bills exceeding ‚Çπ1 lakh

üí° Pro Tip: File Even If Not Mandatory

Even if your income is below the taxable limit, filing ITR has benefits: it serves as income proof for loans, visa applications, and helps claim TDS refunds if excess tax was deducted.

Which ITR Form Should You Use?

Choosing the correct ITR form is essential. Using the wrong form can lead to rejection of your return.

Documents Required for ITR Filing

Keep these documents ready before you start filing:

  • PAN Card - Mandatory for all taxpayers
  • Aadhaar Card - Required for e-verification and linking
  • Form 16 - TDS certificate from employer (for salaried)
  • Form 16A/16B/16C - TDS certificates for other income
  • Form 26AS - Annual Tax Statement (download from TRACES)
  • AIS (Annual Information Statement) - Comprehensive financial transactions
  • Bank Statements - For interest income verification
  • Investment Proofs - For 80C, 80D, 80G deductions
  • Home Loan Certificate - For interest and principal deductions
  • Capital Gains Statements - For stocks, mutual funds, property sales

ITR Filing Due Dates for AY 2026-27

üìÖ Important Due Dates

  • Individuals (Non-Audit): July 31, 2026
  • Businesses (Audit Required): October 31, 2026
  • Transfer Pricing Cases: November 30, 2026
  • Revised/Belated Return: December 31, 2026

Step-by-Step ITR Filing Process

  1. Login to Income Tax Portal: Visit incometax.gov.in and login with your PAN
  2. Download Form 26AS & AIS: Verify all TDS credits and financial transactions
  3. Select Assessment Year: Choose AY 2026-27 for FY 2025-26 income
  4. Choose Correct ITR Form: Based on your income sources
  5. Fill Personal Details: Name, address, bank account for refund
  6. Enter Income Details: Salary, house property, capital gains, other sources
  7. Claim Deductions: 80C, 80D, 80G, HRA exemption, etc.
  8. Calculate Tax Liability: The portal auto-calculates based on regime chosen
  9. Pay Balance Tax (if any): Using challan 280
  10. E-verify Your Return: Using Aadhaar OTP, Net Banking, or DSC

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the penalty for late ITR filing? ‚ñº

If you file after July 31 but before December 31, the penalty is ‚Çπ5,000. If your total income is below ‚Çπ5 lakhs, the penalty is reduced to ‚Çπ1,000. Additionally, you lose the ability to carry forward certain losses and may have to pay interest under Section 234A.

Can I file ITR without Form 16? ‚ñº

Yes, you can file ITR without Form 16. Use your salary slips to calculate income, download Form 26AS to verify TDS credits, and file ITR-1 or ITR-2 based on your income sources. Many freelancers and professionals file without Form 16.

How do I claim TDS refund? ‚ñº

If TDS deducted exceeds your actual tax liability, you'll automatically get a refund after filing ITR. Ensure your bank account details (with IFSC) are correctly entered in the return. Refunds are usually credited within 4-8 weeks after e-verification.

Is ITR filing mandatory for students? ‚ñº

ITR filing is mandatory for students if their income exceeds ‚Çπ3 lakhs (including scholarships, stipends, part-time work, or interest income). Even if income is below the threshold, filing ITR is recommended as it serves as income proof.

Can I revise my ITR after filing? ‚ñº

Yes, you can file a revised return if you discover any mistake or omission in your original return. The revised return must be filed before the end of the relevant assessment year (March 31) or before the completion of assessment, whichever is earlier.

Which tax regime should I choose? ‚ñº

Choose Old Regime if you have significant deductions (80C, HRA, home loan interest totaling more than ‚Çπ3-4 lakhs). Choose New Regime if you have minimal deductions or prefer simpler compliance. Use our tax calculator or consult a CA for personalized advice.
