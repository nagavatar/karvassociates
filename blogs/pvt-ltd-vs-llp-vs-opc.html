Private Limited Company

Best For:

  • Startups planning to raise venture capital or angel investment
  • Businesses with growth ambitions and scaling plans
  • Companies requiring high credibility with clients and vendors
  • Foreign investment or joint ventures

Advantages:

  • Funding: Can issue shares, ESOPs, convertible notes
  • Perpetual Existence: Company continues even if shareholders change
  • Credibility: Banks, clients, and vendors prefer Pvt Ltd
  • Exit Options: Easy to sell company or bring investors

Disadvantages:

  • Higher compliance (Board meetings, AGM, multiple filings)
  • Mandatory statutory audit regardless of turnover
  • Cannot distribute profits freely (dividend rules apply)

Limited Liability Partnership (LLP)

Best For:

  • Professional services (CA, CS, Lawyers, Consultants)
  • Family businesses with 2+ members
  • Businesses not requiring external funding
  • Small to medium enterprises focused on profitability over growth

Advantages:

  • Lower Compliance: Only 2 annual filings (Form 8 & Form 11)
  • No Audit: If turnover < ‚Çπ40 lakhs AND contribution < ‚Çπ25 lakhs
  • Profit Distribution: No dividend tax, partners taxed individually
  • Flexibility: LLP Agreement can be customized

Disadvantages:

  • Cannot raise equity funding (no shares to issue)
  • Limited exit options for partners
  • Higher tax rate (30% flat vs 25% for companies)

One Person Company (OPC)

Best For:

  • Solo entrepreneurs wanting limited liability
  • Freelancers and consultants billing through a company
  • Testing business ideas before scaling
  • Single-person businesses with turnover under ‚Çπ2 crores

Advantages:

  • Single Ownership: Complete control without partners
  • Limited Liability: Personal assets protected
  • Lower Compliance: Compared to regular Pvt Ltd
  • Perpetual Existence: Nominee takes over if needed

Disadvantages:

  • Mandatory Conversion: Must convert to Pvt Ltd if turnover > ‚Çπ2 crores or paid-up capital > ‚Çπ50 lakhs
  • No foreign investment allowed
  • Cannot convert to Section 8 company
  • Only Indian citizen resident can form OPC

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I convert LLP to Private Limited later? ‚ñº

Yes, LLP can be converted to Private Limited Company. The process involves filing forms with ROC, getting new Certificate of Incorporation, and transferring all assets/liabilities. It takes about 30-45 days and requires shareholder agreement on shareholding pattern.

Which has lower annual compliance cost? ‚ñº

LLP has the lowest compliance cost (‚Çπ8,000-15,000/year) as audit is not mandatory below threshold. OPC and Private Limited require mandatory audit (‚Çπ15,000-25,000/year). Private Limited has additional costs for board meetings, AGM documentation, etc.

Can NRI or foreigner be director/partner? ‚ñº

Private Limited: Yes, NRI/foreigner can be director and shareholder (with resident director requirement).
LLP: NRI can be partner with RBI approval; foreigner allowed in permitted sectors.
OPC: Only Indian citizen resident can form OPC.

What happens if OPC crosses ‚Çπ2 crore turnover? ‚ñº

OPC must mandatorily convert to Private Limited Company within 6 months of crossing ‚Çπ2 crore turnover or ‚Çπ50 lakh paid-up capital threshold. This involves adding at least one more director and shareholder, filing conversion forms with ROC.

Can proprietorship be converted to Pvt Ltd/LLP? ‚ñº

Proprietorship cannot be "converted" as it has no separate legal existence. You need to incorporate a new Pvt Ltd or LLP and transfer the business (assets, contracts, employees) to the new entity. This is a business transfer, not conversion.
